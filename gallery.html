Pantone Series

Pantone means all colors. In 1963, Pantone developed the first color matching system. The Pantone Matching System is the color standardization system used widely around the world. The system assists in color matching and identification. It is comprised of 1,867 solid colors that are created by combining 13 base pigments. Every color, in every tone and tint, is given a number to classify it. The colors are assigned a three or four-digit identification number that is followed by the letters U, C, or M. These letters represent paper stocks “uncoated, “coated”, or “matte.”

More than 10 million designers and producers around the world rely on Pantone products and services to help define, communicate, and control color. With these variations, designers and uses can see what their chosen color would look like on each of these different kinds of papers. This helps avoid mistakes like color deviation between the design and the finished product. As long as the designer has the right Pantone number for the color of the product or service, they can be sure about whether or not the color will match specifications.

Pantone’s color language supports all color conscious industries. Pantone B2B Licensing incorporates the Pantone Color System into different products and services, enabling licensees to communicate and reproduce approved Pantone values and improve efficiencies for their users. Pantone brings color and design together. Pantone is a symbol of the design-minded.
